## Eligibility requirements

To register as a contestant for a tertiary team, you must be a student at a tertiary institution in New Zealand, with at least a 50% timetable during the semester of the contest.

There are three categories for tertiary teams.

- **Junior.** Normally students in their first year of programming, who have not taken any second year papers. They would not be particularly fluent yet.
- **Intermediate.** Normally students in their second year of programming, who have not taken any third year papers. They would be reasonably fluent in 1 language.
- **Open.** Tertiary students who do not meet the requirements of the other categories.

A team must compete in the category of the highest rated student, eg a team with two first year students and one second year student must compete as Intermediate.

## Registration

To register, a team requires a team manager who is able to confirm that the contestants meet the eligibility requirements, and will undertake to pay the entry fee for the team (typically $75, but the amount is set per site &ndash; confirm with your site coordinator). Note that there is no fee if the contest is on-line. A staff member would normally fill this role, though a post graduate student could be considered.

Team and contestant registration will be carried out on the contest day before the practice session.

## Teams

A team may contain up to 3 contestants. If space is limited at a site, teams with 3 contestants will be given priority over smaller teams.

Team managers may reserve space for their teams &ndash; usually through TeamUp &ndash; as described on the [Registration page](/register/); check with your site coordinator.
